Balochistan

Balochistan, the largest province of Pakistan, is a vast and rugged land characterized by its diverse landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and strategic importance. Covering almost 44% of the country's total land area.

Sindh

It is bordered by the Pakistani provinces of Balochistan to the west and north-west and Punjab to the north. It shares an International border with the Indian states of Gujarat and Rajasthan to the east; it is also bounded by the Arabian Sea to the south.

Provinces

KPK

Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (KPK), formerly known as the North-West Frontier Province, is a province in Pakistan renowned for its stunning landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and historical significance. Nestled among the foothills of the Himalayas.

Provinces

Punjab

Punjab, the most populous province of Pakistan, is a vibrant and historically rich region that forms the heartland of the country. Known as the "Land of Five Rivers," Punjab derives its name from the Persian words "panj" (five) and "aab" (water).

Provinces

Gilgit Baltistan

Gilgit-Baltistan, located in the northernmost region of Pakistan, is a land of breathtaking landscapes, diverse cultures, and rich history. Nestled amidst the towering peaks of the Himalayas, Karakoram, and Hindu Kush ranges.
