lal Suhanra National Park

Lal-Suhanra National Park is a protected area located in the southern region of Pakistan, specifically in the Bahawalpur District of Punjab. Spanning over 1,400 square kilometers, it is one of the largest national parks in the country and is known for its diverse landscapes and rich biodiversity. The park is named after the Lal and Suhanra rivers, which flow through its heart, and is characterized by its lush forests, rolling hills, and pristine waterways.

It is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna, including rare and endangered species such as the Indus dolphin, Indian pangolin, and Punjab urial. Lal-Suhanra National Park is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, offering a range of activities such as hiking, camping, and wildlife watching. The park's diverse ecosystems provide a unique and challenging experience for visitors.
